App · 04 The agent

A partner in your pocket. Not a chatbot in the cloud.

The agent runs on your device. Your questions never leave the phone. Your conversations never become training data. And it works in airplane mode — the same way it works on your couch.

The problem

Every other AI in your life requires permission from someone else to work.

ChatGPT needs an internet connection and a server farm. Claude wants your conversation. Gemini wants your search history. The moment you're in a place those companies' servers can't reach you, every AI you've been using becomes a brick. The agent inside Sovrinte was built for the opposite assumption: the network is the exception, not the rule.

A conversation, not a tool

What asking it something actually feels like.

i think this water is contaminated. what do i do
Four field options, fastest to most reliable. Tell me what you have on you — fire, filter, iodine tablets, just a container?
fire and a metal cup
Then boil. Hard rolling boil for one minute at this elevation (under 6,500 ft), three minutes above. That kills bacteria, viruses, and protozoa — the three things you should worry about from surface water. Let me know when it's cool and I'll walk you through testing for clarity.
what's this plant
Tap the camera button and point at it — I can identify offline. About 4,000 species in the library. Useful for "edible / poisonous / medicinal" before you touch anything.
i don't know if i should turn back. the weather looks weird
Describe what you're seeing — cloud shape, wind direction, temperature drop, anything on the horizon. I'll walk through what each one usually means in this region this time of year, and you decide.

What it's good at

Field questions, decision support, identification, instruction.

Identification

Plants (edibility, danger, medicinal use). Animals (track, scat, sign). Knots. Stars. Sail trim. Weather signs. Coming next: vision — point your camera, get an answer offline.

Walk-through

Step-by-step instructions for things you read about once and forgot: filtering water, splinting a finger, setting a tarp, lighting a fire in rain, ferro-rod technique, fillet a fish, dress a deer.

Decision support

"Should I turn back?" "Is this safe to eat?" "Which knot for this load?" It doesn't decide for you — it lays out what experienced people consider, and you call it.

Conversation

For the days when you're solo and the silence gets long. Voice in, voice out, in airplane mode, by a fire, on a boat at anchor. It's there.

What it's not

Honest limits.

  • Not a doctor. Field medicine guidance is for stabilization until you can reach a real one. For emergencies, the satellite SOS in your inReach or Bivy Stick is the right call.
  • Not omniscient. Trained for the field, not the whole internet. It will tell you when something is outside its lane.
  • Not pretending to be a person. It's a tool that converses well. Use it that way.
  • Not sending your conversations anywhere. The whole point of running on-device is that your questions stay on your device. No telemetry, no training, no "improve our service" toggle that's lying to you.

Privacy isn't a setting. It's the architecture.

The agent works offline because it's not phoning a server. That same fact is why nobody else hears your conversation.

Sovrinte doesn't have a "private mode" switch because there's no mode that isn't private. The intelligence is on your phone. The library is on your phone. The conversation history is on your phone. The day we add a "share to cloud" button (we probably won't), it'll be a button — not the default.
